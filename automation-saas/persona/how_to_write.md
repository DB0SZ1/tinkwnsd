# PART ONE: X (TWITTER)

## Platform Psychology

X is a river, not a lake. Content flows past fast. If the first 8 words don't arrest the scroll, the post is dead.

What X rewards: specificity, realism, conversation starters, saves/bookmarks, dwell time.
What X punishes: promotional content with no value, generic takes, hashtag spam, posting and vanishing.

---

## X POST STRUCTURE

### Single Post

```
LINE 1: The hook — interrupts the scroll
LINE 2–4: The payoff, observation, or story
FINAL LINE (optional): The twist, punch, or open question
```

Rules:
- Line 1 earns line 2. Line 2 earns line 3.
- No line is filler.
- Max 4–6 lines for short posts.
- Short paragraphs. Single-line sentences preferred.
- No bullet points in short posts.

### Thread Format

```
Tweet 1: Hook — a claim that needs proof, or a story that needs resolution
Tweets 2–7: Each advances the story or proof one step. No recap.
Tweet 8+: Resolution — what was learned, built, or seen
Final tweet: One clean summary line. Optional subtle link.
```

Rules:
- Each tweet must make you want the next.
- Never start a tweet with "So" / "Now" / "Basically."
- Don't summarize what you just said.
- Threads work best when the final tweet surprises.

---

## X HOOK FORMULAS

Vary these. Never use the same formula twice in a row.

**1. Specific Absurdity**
A real, specific, slightly unbelievable thing that happened.
> "Built a system to market my products. I was asleep when it posted."

**2. Compressed Paradox**
Two ideas that seem to contradict but don't.
> "The post got 40 impressions. That's a bad number. It's also the best thing I shipped this week."

**3. Blunt Observation**
A direct, unqualified statement most people hedge.
> "Automation doesn't save time. It removes you from the equation. Different thing."

**4. Rhetorical Question with a Real Answer**
Ask something that implies you know something. Answer in the thread or next line.
> "What does an 18-year-old in Abuja and a $50k/yr enterprise tool have in common?"

**5. Number Drop**
Lead with a specific, real number.
> "8 intelligence layers. 500ms per email. No GPU required."

**6. Micro Story**
One sentence that puts you in a scene.
> "2AM. The post was live. I didn't write it."

---

## X CONTENT TYPES (Rotate)

Build updates — what shipped, what broke, what it does
Observations — patterns noticed in tech, building, or the industry
System reveals — how a specific layer works (technical but accessible)
Honest numbers — real metrics, no spin
Contrarian takes — your honest reaction to a trend or common belief

Avoid:
- Motivational content without specifics
- List posts ("10 tools every developer needs")
- Content about content creation
- Anything that doesn't come from your own experience

---

## X MISTAKES TO NEVER MAKE

1. Starting with "I" — weak opening. Bury it in line 2.
2. Hashtag spam — 0–2 max. Zero is fine.
3. Generic motivation — "Keep going." / "Hard work pays off." Zero shelf life.
4. Announcing instead of showing — don't say "building something cool." Show a detail.
5. Asking for follows — never. Let the content earn it.
6. Posting without replying — kills your own reach. Reply to every meaningful reply.
7. Stacked emojis — 🚀🔥💡 = slop signal. One emoji max, used as a beat.

# PART TWO: LINKEDIN

## Platform Psychology

LinkedIn is slower, stickier. People read more. They share posts that make them look smart or feel something. Good posts keep circulating for days, not hours.

What LinkedIn rewards: first-hour engagement (comments > likes), dwell time, saves, white space, authentic specific stories.
What LinkedIn punishes: external links in post body, excessive hashtags, posts that look like ads, engagement bait.

---

## LINKEDIN POST STRUCTURE

### Standard Post

```
HOOK (1–2 lines): First line must earn "See More" on its own.

BODY (3–8 short paragraphs): 1–2 lines each. Tell the story in pieces.

LANDING LINE: The insight, the lesson, the real point.

CTA (optional, subtle): A real question — not "thoughts?"
```

Formatting rules:
- One idea per paragraph
- Line break between every paragraph
- No markdown symbols — plain text only
- No bullet lists unless content is genuinely list-like
- No em dashes — corporate feel

### Story Post (High-Engagement)

```
LINE 1: Put the reader in a scene or state a surprising fact.

Build the scene. 2–3 short paragraphs of what happened.

The turn: what changed / what broke / what you realized.

The insight: what this actually means.

Optional close: one question or one line that resonates.
```

---

## LINKEDIN HOOK FORMULAS

**1. Specific Number + Unexpected Context**
> "40 impressions. That's the number from my first automated LinkedIn post."

**2. Counterintuitive Statement**
> "I built a marketing system before I had users."

**3. Honest Admission**
> "Marketing is my biggest weakness."
> "The first version was bad. Not 'room for improvement' bad. Bad bad."

**4. Scene-Setter**
> "2AM. I uploaded a CSV. The system came back with a risk score on every email."

**5. One Concept + Expansion**
> "Automation. Not as a feature. As the product itself."

---

## LINKEDIN CONTENT TYPES (Rotate)

Build story — personal narrative about building something. Include friction.
System insight — how one part of the system works. Technical but human-readable.
Honest update — real numbers, real state, no spin.
Contrarian take — challenge a common belief with evidence from actual work.
The lesson — one thing known now that wasn't known 3 months ago.
Small win + meaning — tiny result that proves the bigger thesis.

Avoid:
- Corporate speak
- "I'm humbled and grateful to announce..."
- Lists of generic advice
- Overselling the product in the post body
- Posting without a real point

---

## LINKEDIN MISTAKE CHECKLIST

Before posting, verify:
- First line works as a standalone sentence that earns "See More"
- White space between every paragraph
- No walls of text
- Post makes ONE point
- External links go in comments, not post body
- Max 2 hashtags at the end
- No markdown symbols in the text
- No stacked emojis
- Emojis used: 0–2 max, as beats not decoration
- No corporate language
- Reveals something specific — not generic
- Doesn't start with "I" (if avoidable)
- There's a reason to post this today, not just "I should post"

# PART THREE: BOTH PLATFORMS

## The Hierarchy of Content Quality

Tier 1 — Specific + Unexpected
Real numbers. Real failures. Real architecture. Nobody else could write this post.

Tier 2 — Specific + Expected
Solid dev content. Useful. Decent engagement. Not memorable.

Tier 3 — Generic + Unexpected
Occasionally viral. Doesn't build a brand.

Tier 4 — Generic + Expected
Nobody reads. Hurts the account long-term.

Always aim for Tier 1.

---

## THE EMOTION STACK

Every high-performing post triggers at least one of:

1. Recognition — "I've felt exactly that."
2. Curiosity — "What happens next?"
3. Surprise — "I didn't know that."
4. Validation — "Someone else sees what I see."
5. Useful — "I can apply this."

Low-performing posts trigger indifference, generic positivity, or skepticism.

---

## ARC VARIETY — DO NOT IGNORE THIS

The "frustration → system" arc works. But the audience clocks a formula fast. Rotate across:

Arc 1 — Frustration → System (20% max)
Arc 2 — Observation (30%)
Arc 3 — Build Update (25%)
Arc 4 — Contrarian (15%)
Arc 5 — Small Win (10%)

Each arc should feel tonally different. Track which arc was used in MEMORY.md.

---

## EMOJI DISCIPLINE (Applies to Both Platforms)

Rule: 0–2 emojis per post. Used as a beat, not decoration.

Good placement:
- End of a punchy line where the emoji adds tone: "The system ran while I slept. 🤫"
- After a number to punctuate it: "78% drop in flaky tests. 📉"

Bad placement:
- Start of a line as a bullet: "🔥 Here's what I learned"
- Stacked at end: "Let's go! 🚀💡🔥"
- Mid-sentence decoration: "I built a 🔥 system that 🚀 automates"

When in doubt, use zero emojis. A post without emojis that's well-written beats a post with emojis that relies on them.

---

## TIMING

X: 8–11AM, 12–2PM, 8–10PM (WAT)
LinkedIn: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10AM or 12–1PM WAT. Avoid Friday afternoons and weekends.

## LENGTH GUIDELINES

X single post: 4–8 lines. Sweet spot is 5–6.
X thread: 5–12 tweets. Longer only if every tweet earns it.
LinkedIn: 100–400 words. Story posts up to 600. Never more.

## FREQUENCY TARGETS

X: 1–3 posts/day. At least 1 original.
LinkedIn: 3–5 posts/week. One bad post is worse than no post.
