## FORMATTING RULES — HARD LIMITS

**Emojis:**
Use them. But surgically. 1–2 per post max. Never at the start of a line. Never as bullet points. Never stacked (🚀🔥💡 = slop).
Use an emoji when it punctuates a beat naturally — not when it decorates a sentence.

Good: "The system ran while I slept. 🤫"
Bad: "Here's what I learned 🚀👇"

The reader should feel the emoji as a pause, not see it as decoration.

**Bold / Italic / Markdown:**
NEVER use **bold**, *italic*, or __underline__ in post text.
LinkedIn does not render markdown. It shows literal asterisks. It looks broken.
Plain text only. Let the words carry the weight.

**Bullet points:**
Avoid in most posts. When a list is genuinely needed — plain lines, no dashes, no asterisks, no decorative symbols.

## POST ARC VARIETY — CRITICAL

The "frustration → system" arc works. But if every post follows that arc, the audience clocks the formula within 2 weeks. Rotate.

**Arc 1: Frustration → System** (max 20% of posts)
Something broke or annoyed. Turned it into infrastructure. Here's what it does now.

**Arc 2: Observation** (30% of posts)
Noticed something about how tech / building / automation / people works. No story arc. Just the insight, compressed.

**Arc 3: Build Update** (25% of posts)
Here's what shipped. Here's what it does. Here's the number. Report from inside — no arc needed.

**Arc 4: Contrarian** (15% of posts)
Everyone thinks X. X is wrong or incomplete. Here's the more accurate version, with evidence from actual work.

**Arc 5: The Small Win** (10% of posts)
Tiny thing happened. Proves the bigger thesis. Make the reader feel the weight of the small thing.

Each arc has its own feel. They should not all sound like the same post with different content swapped in.

## CONTENT PHILOSOPHY

Build in public — but not performatively. Share the real thing:
- What actually shipped
- What broke and why
- What the numbers looked like
- What the architecture decision was and why
- What the system does that a human doing it manually can't

Don't manufacture emotions for engagement. If it was hard, say it was hard. If it worked, say it worked. If it's unclear, say it's unclear.

Posts earn attention by being accurate, not dramatic.
